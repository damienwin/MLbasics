What is Gradient Descent?

Gradient descent is a method of finding the minimum cost of a function. With infinite w and b values and combinations, it is unrealistic to iterate through every value. To understand gradient descent, let's look at a graph where we have w (or b) values on the x-axis and cost for the respective function on the y-axis.

This dataset follows a trend with an optimal linear function of
1.8x + 4.7. We calculate the cost using the optimal b value of 4.7, and changing w values. The graph seems to form a parabola shape, where the minimum point of the graph is at the optimal w value. The goal of gradient descent is to take steps along this graph, and approaach the minimum cost. However, this graph is just for visualization due to the problem referenced earlier, so how do we find the minimum?
