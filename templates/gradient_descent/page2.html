Gradient Descent Step Calculation

For simple visualization, only w was graphed in the previous page, however both w and b are updated simutaneously in regression to ensure proper optimization.

To efficiently approach the minimum value, we should adjust the step size: larger steps when farther from the minimum and smaller steps as we get closer. To do this, we derive our cost function. This naturally implements this, as the function's instantaneous derivative, or slope, becomes closer to 0 as it approaches the minimum.

The learning rate controls how fast we approach the minimum. If it is too large, we may skip over the minimum. If it is too small, it may take too long to approach it.
